Choir based in the Polish Community since 2014

ABOUT US

Canonization of two Popes — John XXIII and John Paul II in April 2014, was a splendid occasion for many artists and musical groups all over the world to prepare concerts to commemorate this event.

In Toronto, at the Queen Elizabeth Theatre, the renowned Irish trio The Priests, orchestra Toronto Sinfonietta, and a monumental choir of over 140 singers, under the direction of Matthew Jaskiewicz, gave a fantastic gala performance on May 3rd, 2014.

Although some of the Choir members were representing their home-based groups (i.e. Cantabile Choir, Coro San Marco, Gloria Dei Choir) many Polish singers were not affiliated with any musical group as yet.

What an opportunity!…

This gave Maestro Jaskiewicz chance to create a new choir, not only rooted in the Polish community of Toronto but also open to all choral-singers, regardless of their ethnic background.

A few very energetic people (Mr. & Mrs. C Rumian, and Mr. W. Okulicz) jumped on the idea, and found generous sponsors (the Polish National Union of Canada, with their Chairman, Mr. J. Aleksandrowicz, and the Consulate General of the Republic of Poland in Toronto), and in September 2014, the newly created choir under the name of Novi Singers started rehearsing.

Wednesday evenings between 8:00 p.m. and 10:00 p.m. are “noisy” in the building of the Polish National Union of Canada at 71 Judson Street.

Vocal exercises and beautiful 4-part harmonies are enthusiastically practised by our about 40-people ensemble, still open to more members!

Music lovers with good voice and musical ear, preferably with some previous choral singing experience and musical background are welcome.

In conclusion - Novi Singers Toronto have already marked their existence on the cultural scene of the Toronto “Polonia”. They have every reason to prevail, develop and grow!

Come sing with us! Give us a try!

OUR ARTISTIC TEAM

Biographies

MATTHEW JASKIEWICZ

ARTISTIC DIRECTOR

Matthew Jaskiewicz is a graduate of the Warsaw Academy of Music, where he studied with the renowned conductor Bohdan Wodiczko. He launched his professional career with the directorship of several prestigious ensembles in Poland and in France.

They include the Academic Choir of Warsaw University, the Ars Antiqua Chamber Choir, and Les Rossignolets de Roubaix boys’ choir. While still in Warsaw, Matthew taught choral conducting at the Warsaw Music Academy and was the conductor of the Warsaw Chamber Opera for many years.

Through extensive touring in Europe, Mr. Jaskiewicz has earned an international distinction and many significant awards, among them the First Prize at the International Choral Festival in Middlesborough, England.

Following his move to Canada in 1984, Matthew founded the Polish Canadian Society of Music and its flagship ensembles, the Toronto Sinfonietta orchestra and Musica Antiqua Vocal Ensemble.

Since its establishment as an independent organization, Toronto Sinfonietta is being recognized for its innovative programming, commissioning of original works by Canadian composers, and development of young musicians.

In addition to his tenure as the Artistic Director of Toronto Sinfonietta, Matthew currently conducts the Oakham House Choir of Ryerson University. For many years, he served as an Artistic Director of Northumberland Choir and Orchestra in Cobourg, the Polish “Symphonia Choir” in Hamilton, Baycrest Music Society Choir, and The Angel Choir of the Chinese community in Toronto.

In 2006, Matthew Jaskiewicz established Toronto Sinfonietta Concerto Competition for young musicians under the age of 19, culminating in an Annual Gala Concert of Young Musicians.

In recognition of his cultural achievements, he was awarded the prestigious Polish Order of Merit in 1996. In 2006, he was presented with the Knight’s Cross of The Order of Polonia Restituta, and in 2011, he received the Gold Medal Gloria Artis, the highest award bestowed on artists by the president of Poland.

KONRAD GAJEWSKI

REHEARSAL PIANIST

Konrad is currently studying music at the University of Toronto, Faculty of Music under prof. Adam Zukiewicz.

He has been playing piano since the age of five, and holds the Royal Conservatory of Music ARCT Certificate in Piano Performance.

Konrad has received various awards and scholarships including: Kiwanis Festival, Young Talent's Concert, EuroMusic, Burlington and Davenport piano competitions.

Apart from his solo endeavours, Konrad has been enjoying a position of an accompanist with the Novi Singers Toronto since September 2014.
